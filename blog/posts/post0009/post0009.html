Service

July 12th 2025

I'm currently listening to “How To Win Friends And Influence People” by Dale Carnegie, after watching a podcast interview with John Paul DeJoria, the founder of Paul Mitchell Systems (hair care) and Patron (tequila), who recommended this book as such a good asset along his journey to amassing billions of dollars. It is quite the wake-up call to me, in communication, that even in my (to me) sincere attempts to be selfless and of pure intent, I realise now how little I have really been striking through the mask to do so, and have been quite consumed in the mask of my ego, leading the sincere side of me to believe that I have been acting selflessly, but in fact I haven’t, and there is ample room to do more.

It reminds me of my previous impulses towards service toward the elderly, left in old dusty places of my mind for various reasons, perhaps because it was not appropriate, and that I need to make more money… Well, I’m not making money now, even after months of trying. So, perhaps the issue lies in the lack of service I am really offering people. Perhaps I need to simply give more and ask for less, even if I sincerely believe I deserve more. Perhaps, it’s up to me to pick up this old impulse toward service and to give it a second shot, outside of institutions, but to simply do it for the joy that comes from filling the needs of others.

I have been contemplating and brainstorming ways to implement the concept of “one man’s trash is another man’s treasure” to my advantage, almost quite literally, seeking to find where the richer folk dispose of their literal garbage, and to maybe catch some crumbs of gold. But perhaps the “trash” is a lot closer to home. Perhaps that gold may be found in the “waste of time” experiences of giving service to others, when I myself am in need. Perhaps, betting on luck is better than betting on others’ 1 in a million chance of randomly giving me a job, as my current odds seem to be.

Perhaps, if I go out and give service, and show the value that I know that I have, that it will no longer be a debate, and it will be solidified both to myself and others that I do in fact put others above myself and have that within me. And that what I am really passionate about is others, collaboration, and humanity as a whole. I have deleted every other post on this blog, for I do not wish to hold onto the past in such a way. All of that is merely prelude to what is currently important. That is to get out of my ego and my own problems, and to lose said ego in someone else’s.

I’m looking to find elderly to help. We are so divided, so I think I will continue along this path in order to reach them no matter what. I am quite uptight in that I don’t like going through institutions, but I think that’s fine. I’m sure there are elderly out there that will appreciate that I do not want our interactions to simply be a business transaction, which it can so often feel (and often actually be) these days. There is nothing wrong with human-to-human interaction, which may contradict what we were taught during COVID. But that’s fine, because the truth is, there is nothing wrong with us, nor our interactions. And though it took a little while for that truth to resurface, I believe we are on the right track.

Yours truly,
